The **Control Tower AB** sits across your supplier commits, carrier capacity updates, and customer commitments. It reconciles them into one live state and flags the moment a commit slips — then drafts the escalation memo before anyone has to ask.

No manual trigger. It's always running.

## AB · Control Tower

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **Watches** | Supplier portals · carrier TMS · inbound ETAs · PCNs |
| **Triggers** | Commit slip · capacity shift · lead-time delta |
| **Owns** | Freight · packaging · supplier pipelines |
| **Outputs** | Live state · weekly digest · escalation memo |

## How it works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Control Tower monitors continuously">
    The AB reconciles allocation, capacity, and commitment states in real time across supplier portals, carrier TMS, and your ERP. No manual trigger.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Commit slip detected">
    The AB identifies the SKU, shipment, or freight lane. It flags the slip, categorizes severity, and drafts the escalation memo.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Memo posted in your channel">
    The draft appears in your Slack, Teams, WhatsApp, or Telegram channel. Review it inline.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Approve and escalate">
    Reply to approve. The AB sends the memo and logs the event with a full chain of custody.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  Commit slip detected to escalation memo sent: under 2 minutes.
</Info>

<Note>
  Delay detection and allocation reconciliation run automatically. The Control Tower AB also owns your weekly allocation review — the one previously done in a spreadsheet.
</Note>
