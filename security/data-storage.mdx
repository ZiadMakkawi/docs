The Brain runs on hardware that physically lives inside your office, behind your firewall. Your allocation data, supplier contracts, export memos — they do not leave the room.

## What stays on your machine

- Supplier commits and allocation states
- Carrier capacity data and portal credentials
- Proof of delivery records
- Deduction sheets and credit note PDFs
- Export screening memos and compliance citations
- Every document an AB reads or writes
- Full audit log with cryptographic chain of custody

## What leaves your machine

Only the **task instruction** — the message your team sends, or the trigger the AB fires on.

Your actual operational data stays local. In cloud-connected deployments, the AI provider receives the instruction, not the content. In air-gap mode, nothing leaves at all.

## Encryption

| Layer | Standard | What it covers |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **At rest** | AES-256 · Secure Enclave | Logs · task history · documents · credentials · config |
| **In transit** | TLS | Between Brain and AI provider (cloud-connected deployments only) |

Credentials are sealed against a key that never leaves the appliance's secure element — FileVault-class disk encryption with hardware-backed keys.

## Air-gap mode

For export-controlled customers, the Brain runs without internet egress. Models, weights, and tooling are pre-baked into the appliance and updated by signed image. Zero calls out. Nothing transmitted.

```bash
# Enable air-gap mode during onboarding
leedab onboard --air-gap
```
